Animus — Type Exploration 02

Outfit vs DM Sans — with serif pairings

Focused comparison of the two finalists. Lora stays as our anchor serif. Two new serif options join: Crimson Pro and EB Garamond. All set with brand copy, both palettes, and pairing compositions.

Head to Head — Key differences
Outfit
animus
Geometric with rounded, open forms. The 'a' is single-story (round). The 'n' and 'u' have soft shoulders. Inherently warm and friendly. Slightly wider letterforms give it an airy, generous feel.
DM Sans
animus
Geometric with more structure. The 'a' is double-story at text sizes (optically adjusted). More compact and refined. Feels quietly confident — less friendly, more authoritative. The optical sizing axis adapts letterforms for each size.

Serif Companions

Lora + two new options

Lora stays as our anchor. Crimson Pro brings refinement with warmth — think of it as Lora's more elegant sibling. EB Garamond brings old-world warmth with real organic character, rooted in one of the most beloved type traditions. Both are balanced and well-formed — no wonky letters.

Lora
Something that lives alongside you.
Animus is not just software. It's the beginning of something that lives alongside you. A mind that persists, a spirit that adapts, a will that acts.
Warm, well-rooted. Moderate contrast with brushed curves. Trustworthy and grounded — excellent body readability. The most "bookish" of the three. Feels reliable and human.
Crimson Pro
Something that lives alongside you.
Animus is not just software. It's the beginning of something that lives alongside you. A mind that persists, a spirit that adapts, a will that acts.
Refined, slightly higher contrast than Lora. Clean and elegant without being cold. Variable font with a wide weight range. More modern than Lora — feels like the typography of a well-designed product, not a novel.
EB Garamond
Something that lives alongside you.
Animus is not just software. It's the beginning of something that lives alongside you. A mind that persists, a spirit that adapts, a will that acts.
Old-world beauty with organic warmth. Based on the Garamond tradition — one of the most human typeface families ever designed. Slightly narrower, with beautiful italic forms. The most "alive" feeling of the three — letters feel shaped by a hand, not a grid.
