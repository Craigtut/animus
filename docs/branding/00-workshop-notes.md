# Animus Brand Workshop — Session Notes

## Strategic Decisions

### Audience & Context
- **Primary context:** Consumer product
- Brand needs to work at scale: app stores, marketing sites, social media, product UI
- Mark must be emotionally resonant, not just legible
- Should hold from 16px favicon to hero/billboard scale

### Typography — LOCKED
- **Primary sans-serif:** Outfit (replaces Plus Jakarta Sans)
  - Warm geometric with rounded, open forms
  - Inherently friendly without being childish
  - Single-story 'a', soft shoulders, generous width
  - Full weight range: 200–700
- **Display serif:** Crimson Pro
  - Refined, elegant, variable font
  - Higher contrast than Lora — more modern
  - Used for hero headlines, editorial moments, display scale
  - Pairs beautifully with Outfit — sophistication meets warmth
- **Monospace:** JetBrains Mono / Fira Code (unchanged, for code contexts)
- **Wordmark:** Lowercase "animus" — approachable, confident, no need to shout

### Typography — Eliminated
- Plus Jakarta Sans — too clinical, especially at thin weights
- Nunito — too soft, lacks sophistication
- DM Sans — strong contender but Outfit won on warmth
- Lora — warm but Crimson Pro is more refined
- EB Garamond — beautiful but narrower, slightly too traditional
- Fraunces — too messy, M and N have unbalanced slants
- Instrument Serif — too editorial/cold

### Symbol Direction
- Exploring multiple directions — not yet locked
- Brand vision anchors: breath, pulse, spirit, emergence, continuous flow
- Must feel organic, not geometric or digital
- Capable of animation (breathing, shifting)
- Sophisticated but approachable
