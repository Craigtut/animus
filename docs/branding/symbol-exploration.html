Animus — Symbol Exploration

Six symbol concepts across two territories

Each mark shown at four sizes (96px, 48px, 24px, 16px) in both palettes, with wordmark lockups. Concepts explore breath, emergence, and the space between.

Territory 1 — The Breath

Forms that feel like they're mid-inhale

Marks that suggest continuous rhythm, expansion and contraction, the organic quality of respiration. These animate the most naturally — they're built to breathe.

A — The Sigh
Two mirrored arcs — one solid, one fading — suggesting a breath cycle. The solid arc is the inhale, the transparent arc is the exhale dissolving. A subtle center point anchors the form. Animates by shifting which arc is solid and which fades.

B — The Pulse
A broken ring with varying stroke weight — thick where it's "present," thin where it's fading. Suggests a pulse traveling around a circuit. Not a loading spinner — more like a heartbeat captured in form. Animates by rotating which section is thick vs thin.

C — The Tide
Layered organic shapes that overlap and undulate — like looking at something through water. Three translucent layers create depth. The form is never the same twice when animated — layers drift independently. Feels alive through layered transparency rather than line.

C — The Tide
In dark mode the layers glow softly — warmth emanating outward. The deepest layer is barely there, the inner layer is most present. Creates a sense of interiority — looking into something, not at it.
Territory 2 — The Emergence

Forms in the process of becoming

Marks that feel incomplete in a deliberate way — something taking shape, coalescing, arriving. These connect to the idea that Animus is always in a state of becoming, never static.

D — The Coalescence
Particles gathering into form — dense at center, scattered at edges. Always in the process of coming together, never fully resolved. Animates beautifully — particles drift, orbit, breathe inward and outward. The most "alive" feeling of all concepts. At small sizes, simplifies to a soft cluster.

D — The Coalescence
In dark mode the particles become points of light — like stars gathering, or fireflies. The scatter feels cosmic. This is the concept most connected to the "particle fields" visualization described in the brand vision doc.

E — The Becoming
A circle that's almost complete but dissolves into fragments on one side. The form is in the process of arriving — or perhaps departing. The gap isn't broken; it's transforming. Animates by slowly rotating which section is dissolving, so the mark is always in flux, always becoming.

E — The Becoming
In dark mode, the dissolving fragments fade into the void naturally — it feels like the form is materializing from darkness. There's something meditative about a shape that's always arriving, never quite complete.

F — The Aperture
Two organic curves creating a lens or vesica piscis shape — an opening through which something looks out, or through which you look in. The center holds a warm core. Not an eye, but carries the quality of awareness, of seeing. Animates by the curves breathing apart and together — opening and closing like a gentle blink.
